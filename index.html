A large-scale benchmark and reasoning-enhanced forensic framework for detecting semantically coherent, MLLM-driven multimodal forgeries.

Abstract

Multimodal misinformation detection and grounding are critical for safeguarding digital media security and public trust in the era of generative AI. Existing multimodal forensic frameworks mainly address rule-based or semantically misaligned forgeries, but overlook an emerging threat: modern Multimodal Large Language Models can generate fluent narratives that remain semantically consistent with manipulated images. Such coordinated image-text fabrications closely resemble realistic adversarial behavior, deceive human observers, and weaken detectors that rely on cross-modal inconsistency.

To systematically study this threat, we introduce ROM, a large-scale benchmark containing more than 704K semantically aligned multimodal samples across five news domains. ROM covers diverse face-level and scene-level visual forgeries, text fabrication, and their combinations. Each manipulated sample is paired with a fluent yet misleading narrative and augmented with fine-grained manipulation labels, localization annotations, and explicit forensic reasoning chains.

We further propose REFORM++, a reasoning-enhanced forensic framework that reformulates manipulation detection and grounding as a unified question-answering task. REFORM++ combines dual-decoder forensic reasoning, artifact-adaptive graph aggregation, and reasoning-cultivation reinforcement learning. Experiments on ROM, DGM4, and MMFakeBench demonstrate strong cross-domain and zero-shot generalization to unseen forgery patterns and out-of-domain media.

Existing benchmarks often construct visual and textual manipulations independently, leaving obvious semantic mismatches that can be exploited as detection shortcuts. ROM instead conditions an MLLM on the manipulated image and editing metadata to generate a fluent but misleading narrative that remains consistent with the visual content.

This coordinated construction creates a coherence trap: image-text agreement can no longer be treated as evidence of authenticity. A reliable detector must move beyond superficial semantic alignment and examine intrinsic visual artifacts, factual plausibility, and the logical consistency between forensic evidence and its final verdict.

Overview

Project Summary

Existing multimodal detectors often treat image-text inconsistency as the primary evidence of manipulation. This assumption breaks down when an MLLM rewrites the caption after visual editing: the image and text can remain semantically consistent while jointly supporting a fabricated event. ROM benchmarks this coordinated attack setting, while REFORM++ learns to detect, localize, and explain the underlying manipulation through structured forensic reasoning.

The Coherence Trap

Cross-modal agreement is not necessarily evidence of authenticity. In ROM, visual edits and MLLM-generated narratives are deliberately coordinated, removing the shortcut of obvious semantic mismatch and forcing detectors to inspect intrinsic artifacts, factual plausibility, and fine-grained forensic evidence.

What ROM provides

ROM contains real news and nine forgery categories covering Face Swap, Face Attribute, Background Replacement, Full Generation, Text Fabrication, and their multimodal combinations. It provides authenticity labels, fine-grained manipulation labels, image grounding annotations, and forensic reasoning supervision across five news domains.

What REFORM++ predicts

REFORM++ generates a structured output containing the real/fake verdict, fine-grained image and text manipulation types, and manipulated-region coordinates when applicable. Its parallel reasoning branch explains the forensic evidence supporting the final answer.

Dataset

ROM Benchmark

ROM is constructed from a source-news pool containing more than 2.9 million real-world image-text pairs. It models coordinated multimodal attacks by first applying face-level or scene-level visual manipulation and then using an MLLM to generate a fluent narrative that remains consistent with the manipulated image.

The benchmark contains one real category and nine manipulation categories: Face Swap, Face Swap with Text Fabrication, Face Attribute, Face Attribute with Text Fabrication, Background Replacement, Background Replacement with Text Fabrication, Full Generation, Full Generation with Text Fabrication, and Text Fabrication. Quality filtering is applied to preserve visual fidelity, semantic alignment, and narrative realism.

Framework

REFORM++

REFORM++ treats multimodal manipulation detection, fine-grained type recognition, and spatial grounding as a unified question-answering problem. Rather than using separate task-specific prediction heads, the model produces a structured textual answer containing the authenticity decision, manipulation types, and grounding coordinates.

A parallel reasoning branch is trained to articulate the forensic evidence behind the answer. The framework progressively cultivates this capability through cognitive reasoning warm-up, joint reason-answer fine-tuning, and constraint-aware policy refinement.

Unified Forensic Question Answering

Detection, manipulation-type recognition, and grounding are represented in one structured answer, enabling a unified and extensible task formulation.

Cognitive Priming and Dual Decoding

A Cognitive Priming Encoder activates manipulation-aware evidence, while separate Reason and Answer Decoders model the forensic rationale and final decision in parallel.

Reason-Answer Alignment

The Reason-Answer Consistency objective encourages the generated rationale and final verdict to express semantically compatible forensic conclusions.

Constraint-Aware Policy Refinement

GRPO optimizes accuracy, grounding quality, output format, and reason-answer consistency, encouraging both correct predictions and logically aligned explanations.

REFORM++ extends REFORM with Artifact-Adaptive Graph Aggregation and refined reinforcement-learning sample selection. AAGA explicitly models correlations among sparsely distributed visual and textual forensic traces, while the refined sampling strategy combines challenging error-prone cases with reliable high-confidence examples for more stable policy optimization.

Visualization

Qualitative Results

REFORM++ provides transparent forensic outputs across real news, face-level manipulation, scene-level synthesis, and text fabrication. For each input, the model generates an explicit reasoning chain followed by a structured answer containing the authenticity verdict, fine-grained manipulation type, and grounding coordinates when applicable.

Human Assessment of Reasoning Quality

Human evaluation further confirms the interpretability of REFORM++. Across five news domains, 85.8% of the generated reasoning chains are rated as either Acceptable or Highly Convincing, while only 5.5% are rated as Not Convincing.

Failure Analysis
  • Logical Hallucination: the verdict is correct, but the rationale relies on invalid temporal or factual logic.
  • Semantic-Dominant Attention Masking: a strong caption contradiction can distract the model from a simultaneous visual forgery.
  • Sensory Limits in Low-Fidelity Imagery: severe noise, blur, or compression can suppress the subtle traces required for reliable artifact inspection.
  • Visual Perception Failure in OOD Domains: cinematic or out-of-distribution imagery can induce hallucinated scene understanding and missed evidence.
